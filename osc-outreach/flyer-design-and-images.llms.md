**Printy**

An affordable external option for printing (especially for posters) is [Printy](https://printy.de/en/homepage-english-2/)

Location: near TUM Main Campus, Luisenstraße 49, 80333 Munich  
Opening hours: Monday-Friday 9:30 AM – 5:00 PM

Typical process with Printy:

- On their website, go to “Price calculators” → “large poster calculator”.
- Leave the default settings that are automatically chosen (A0 format, 145 gr. silkmatt in & outdoor presentations, pickup Luisenstr.49, receipt).
- Click “Request Order” which will open an email window with pre-drafted text containing your order details.
- Upload a PDF version of your poster in this email and edit/add to the email where necessary.
- Send the email and expect a reply (usually within the same day) to collect your poster.

For reference, an A0 poster here costs around €15,00

## PowerPoint

**Using Microsoft PowerPoint for Image Creation**

We often use PowerPoint to create images or logos for various uses. Here are some helpful tips:

- It is possible to resize the page by going to the Design tab → click on “Slide Size” → set the size with “Custom Slide Size…”
- When importing images onto the slide, changing the color of that image may not always work perfectly (the color can be slightly off from what you select). The best format to get the most accurate color change is SVG. There are many websites with free-to-use icons that can be exported as SVG images, such as <https://iamvector.com/>. First, save the image as an SVG on your device, then import it into the slides.
- SVG is the best format for maintaining high-quality images on the website. Even with multiple slides, you can save a specific slide as an SVG image.

> **TIP:**
>
> Make sure you are on the slide you want to save → go to the File tab → click on “Save As” → change the format from .pptx to Scalable Vector Graphics Format (.svg) → click “Save” → a dialogue box will pop up, and you should select “Just This One”.
